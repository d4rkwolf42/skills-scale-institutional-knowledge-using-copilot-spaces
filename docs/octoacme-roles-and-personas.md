# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on process adherence. They serve as the point of contact for sprint health and continuously support process improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments for the development team
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Protect the team from scope creep and external distractions
- Track sprint metrics and health indicators
- Escalate risks and dependencies to the Project Manager when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate on timeline, dependencies, and escalations; provide early warning on risks
- **Developers:** Support technical team's ability to deliver; facilitate process improvements
- **Product Managers:** Align on sprint scope and story prioritization

### Goals
- Maintain consistent sprint velocity and predictability
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration
- Reduce cycle time and dependencies
- Support continuous process improvement

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Weekly sync with PM on sprint health and impediments
- Retrospective notes and action items
- Process documentation and team guidelines

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own the usability, accessibility, and visual quality of the product. They gather user feedback, translate requirements into intuitive interfaces, and ensure consistency across the user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather feedback on usability and design
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine requirements and user stories
- Work with Developers to ensure design fidelity and technical feasibility
- Establish and maintain design systems and style guides
- Validate solutions through user testing and iteration

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers:** Define user needs and success metrics; validate design decisions
- **Developers:** Ensure design implementation matches specifications; discuss technical constraints
- **QA Lead:** Verify visual design and usability acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent user experiences
- Reduce support tickets related to user confusion or usability issues
- Establish design best practices and consistency across projects

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with stakeholders
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria refinement
- Handoff documentation for developers
- User research findings and design rationale

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads establish quality standards, define testing strategies, and coordinate testing activities (manual and automated). They serve as the primary contact for quality concerns and ensure acceptance criteria are met before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and quality acceptance criteria for features
- Coordinate automated and manual testing activities
- Identify gaps in test coverage and propose improvements
- Manage test data, environments, and tools
- Track and triage bugs, ensuring priority alignment
- Serve as the primary escalation point for quality issues
- Validate readiness for release against quality standards

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Collaborate on unit test coverage, test automation, and test data setup
- **Project Managers:** Flag quality risks and blockers; coordinate testing timelines
- **Product Managers:** Define acceptance criteria and validation approaches
- **Release Manager:** Ensure quality sign-off before production deployment

### Goals
- Catch defects early and reduce production incidents
- Maintain consistent quality standards across releases
- Build confidence in the release process through thorough testing
- Support continuous improvement of testing practices

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and acceptance criteria documentation
- Daily standup updates on testing status
- Bug reports and quality metrics
- Pre-release quality sign-off

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan and oversee all release-related activities, ensuring readiness for deployment and coordinating cross-team communications. They manage the entire release lifecycle from planning through post-deployment verification.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases (patches, minor, major)
- Ensure all pre-release requirements are met (testing, documentation, approvals)
- Coordinate release communications across technical and non-technical teams
- Manage deployment to staging and production environments
- Execute rollback procedures if issues arise during deployment
- Document release notes and migration steps
- Conduct post-deployment verification and stakeholder announcements
- Track release metrics and continuous improvement opportunities

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate release timeline and stakeholder communication
- **QA Lead:** Verify quality sign-off and acceptance criteria completion
- **Developers:** Coordinate code freezes, deployment scripts, and rollback procedures
- **Product Managers:** Ensure feature readiness and success metric setup

### Goals
- Execute predictable, low-risk releases with minimal downtime
- Maintain clear communication with all stakeholders
- Enable rapid rollback and incident response if needed
- Reduce time-to-market for new features and fixes

### Typical Communication
- Release plans and deployment windows
- Pre-release checklists and sign-offs
- Deployment runbooks and rollback procedures
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Post-deployment metrics and incident reports

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical execution. They gather and clarify requirements, manage documentation, and support alignment between stakeholders and delivery teams throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria
- Identify and document dependencies, assumptions, and constraints
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder discussions
- Maintain requirement traceability and change logs
- Support scope definition and trade-off discussions
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers:** Refine business objectives, prioritize requirements, validate solutions
- **Project Managers:** Define scope, manage change requests, communicate trade-offs
- **Developers:** Clarify technical feasibility and implementation approaches
- **Stakeholders:** Gather requirements, validate understanding, communicate status

### Goals
- Ensure clear, shared understanding of requirements across all team members
- Reduce scope creep and requirement-related rework
- Improve stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Enable data-driven decision-making on priorities and trade-offs

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specification sheets
- Stakeholder workshops and interviews
- User story refinement sessions
- Requirement traceability matrices
- Change request analysis and impact assessments
